# Hi, I'm Niklas Knobel

I am a mathematics researcher with a background in  PDEs, asymptotic methods, mathematical modelling, stochastic analysis, and machine learning. My current focus is on applying rigorous mathematical and statistical methods to quantitative finance, systematic investing, and market microstructure.

I completed my PhD in Mathematics at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology and am currently a Research Associate at Imperial College London. My academic work focuses on stability, instability, nonlinear dynamics, and perturbative methods in fluid-type equations.

## Quantitative finance projects

### Empirical ML Equity Return Prediction
A Python research pipeline for next-month equity return prediction using lagged returns, volatility, and market beta features.

- Built a monthly stock-level prediction dataset from historical market data
- Compared Ridge, Random Forest, Extra Trees, XGBoost, and LightGBM models
- Used rolling 20-year training windows with annual refitting
- Evaluated forecasts through out-of-sample prediction metrics and long-short portfolio backtests

Repository: [ML-asset-pricing](https://github.com/Niklas6/ML-asset-pricing)

### Avellaneda-Stoikov Market-Making Simulation
A simulation framework for inventory-aware market making under stochastic price dynamics and exponential order arrivals.

- Implemented fixed-spread, symmetric, and inventory-aware market-making agents
- Ran Monte Carlo simulations to compare PnL, inventory risk, and Sharpe ratio
- Studied the effect of inventory control on strategy stability and risk-adjusted performance

Repository: [AvellanedaStoikov](https://github.com/Niklas6/AvellanedaStoikov)
